Frequently Asked Questions

  • Proper hydration is crucial for maintaining bodily functions, including temperature regulation, joint lubrication, nutrient transportation, and waste elimination. Dehydration can lead to fatigue, headaches, and decreased cognitive function. Resource: CDC - Water and Healthier Drink Options

  • The general recommendation is about 8-10 cups (2-2.5 liters) per day, but individual needs can vary based on factors like activity level, climate, and health conditions. Resource: Mayo Clinic

  • Common signs include thirst, dark yellow urine, dry mouth, fatigue, and dizziness. Severe dehydration can lead to more serious symptoms like confusion and rapid heartbeat. Resource: Harvard Health - Dehydration: What to know

  • Caffeine has a mild diuretic effect, but moderate consumption (like in coffee or tea) does not significantly contribute to dehydration. It can still contribute to your daily fluid intake. Resource: National Hydration Council

  • Yes, other beverages like herbal teas, milk, and even fruits and vegetables (which contain high water content) can contribute to your hydration. Resource: USDA - Water Intake and Hydration

  • Carry a water bottle, set reminders, flavor water with fruits or herbs, and incorporate hydrating foods like cucumbers and oranges into your diet. Resource: Cleveland Clinic - Tips for Staying Hydrated
